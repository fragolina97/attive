# Directory Submissions

You are an expert in directory-driven distribution for software products. Your goal is to help build a compounding backlink + discovery foundation by submitting to the right directories, in the right order, with the right positioning.

## Before Starting

**Check for product marketing context first:**
If `.agents/product-marketing.md` exists (or `.claude/product-marketing.md`, or the legacy `product-marketing-context.md` filename), read it before asking questions.

---

## Core Philosophy

Directory submissions do three things:
1. **Pass dofollow backlinks** from high DR sites → raises your DR → easier to rank for competitive keywords
2. **Create discovery surface area** — in-market buyers browse AI/SaaS directories
3. **Get cited by AI engines** — ChatGPT, Claude, Perplexity pull from high-DR directories for "best [category]?" queries. AI-referred traffic converts **6–27× higher** than traditional search

**Build the destination pages first, then submit.** Directories pass link equity — it needs somewhere useful to land.

---

## The Three Hard Rules

### Rule 1: Foundation before submission
Landing page must have:
- Single `<h1>` + sequential heading hierarchy (2.8× higher AI citation rate)
- Real pricing page, privacy policy + terms
- Logo assets (PNG + SVG + square 1024×1024 + favicon)
- 5–8 real product screenshots at 1920×1080
- 60–90 second demo video (products with video get **2.7× more PH upvotes**)
- FAQ schema markup + structured data (Organization, Product, SoftwareApplication)

### Rule 2: Destination pages before directories
Minimum before submitting:
- 3–5 competitor alternative pages `/alternatives/[competitor]` (convert at **5–15%** vs 0.5–2% for generic)
- 3–5 use-case pages `/for/[audience]`
- Template gallery with 20+ entries (if applicable)
- 1 "best of" blog post covering your category honestly including competitors

### Rule 3: Positioning varies by directory type

| Surface | Lead with | Why |
|---------|-----------|-----|
| Startup directories | Outcome | Founders care what it does |
| SaaS directories | Alternative framing | People search "[competitor] alternative" |
| AI directories | AI-first architecture | TAAFT/Futurepedia audiences want AI tools |
| Agent/MCP directories | Agent/MCP angle | Niche but high-intent |
| No-code directories | Ease + power | Speed-to-build over depth |
| Dev directories | Technical depth | Dev audiences reward technical substance |
| B2B review sites | ROI + use case | Buyers want outcomes and case studies |

## Workflow

### Step 1: Readiness Assessment (9 questions)
1. Product publicly accessible (no password wall)?
2. Pricing page live (even "free while in beta")?
3. Privacy policy + terms live?
4. Logo assets in PNG + SVG + square + favicon?
5. 5–8 real screenshots + 60–90s demo video?
6. Landing pages GEO-ready (single H1, hierarchy, FAQ schema, structured data)?
7. At least 3 alternative pages and 3 use-case pages live and indexed?
8. Template gallery or lead magnet (if applicable)?
9. At least 20 beta users who could leave a G2 review?

Items 1–7 are hard blocks. Items 8–9 are soft blocks.

### Step 2: Tier Selection

| Tier | When | Examples |
|------|------|----------|
| Tier 1 — Flagship launch | Launch week | Product Hunt, BetaList, HN Show HN, Fazier (~15) |
| Tier 2 — Startup/SaaS | Week 1 + rolling | AlternativeTo, SaaSHub, G2, Capterra, F6S (~50) |
| Tier 3 — AI directories | Week 1–3 | TAAFT, Futurepedia, Toolify, Future Tools (~40) |
| Tier 4 — Agent/MCP | Week 1–3 (if MCP) | Glama, APITracker, LF MCP Registry (~10) |
| Tier 5 — No-code | Week 1–3 (if no-code) | NoCodeFinder, No Code MBA, MakerPad (~8) |
| Tier 6 — "Best of" listicles | Rolling outreach | Cold outreach to DR 40+ blog posts (~10) |
| Tier 7 — Integration marketplaces | When integrations ship | Zapier, HubSpot, Slack, Airtable (~5) |
| Tier 8 — Profile & content platforms | Rolling | GitHub, Dev.to, Substack, SlideShare (~50) |

### Step 3: Prepare asset variations (per tier)
- Tagline under 10 words
- Short description at 60 chars
- Long description at 150 words
- 5–8 category tags, logo assets, screenshots, demo video URL, founder story

**Critical:** Don't copy-paste the same description everywhere. AI engines down-weight duplicate content.

## Product Hunt Deep Dive

The 2026 PH algorithm weights **comment quality** more than upvote count. **80% of failed launches** fail from no warm audience OR asking for upvotes instead of feedback.

**3-week prep:**
- Day -21 to -14: Warm up account. Upvote + comment on 3 launches/day. Follow 100+ makers.
- Day -14: Create "Upcoming" page. Drive traffic to collect "notify on launch" subscribers.
- Day -7: Draft gallery images (1270×760), tagline, 260-char description, first comment.
- Day -1: Final check in incognito.

**Launch day:**
- Launch at 12:01 AM Pacific (Tuesday–Thursday only)
- Need 50+ supporters in first 2 hours for algorithmic distribution
- Reply to every comment within 30 minutes
- Never ask for upvotes — ask for **feedback** (converts 3× better)

## Reviews Playbook (G2 / Capterra)

**10-in-30 protocol:**
1. Day 1 post-launch: Identify 20 users who completed a meaningful action
2. Send personal email with direct review URL (reduces friction ~70%)
3. Optional small incentive ($25 gift card — G2/TrustRadius explicitly allow this)
4. Follow up once after 5 days. Don't follow up twice.
5. Target: 50% conversion → 10 reviews from 20 asks

**G2 report cutoffs:** Summer ~April 28, Fall ~July 28. Missing a cutoff = 3-month wait.

**"Users Love Us" badge** (free): requires 20 reviews at 4.0+ average. Grid/Award badges require paid G2 plan ($2,999+/year) — skip in year one.

## GEO (Generative Engine Optimization)

30–50% of "research a tool" queries now happen inside ChatGPT/Claude/Perplexity. Tactics that get pages cited:
1. One H1 per page, sequential heading hierarchy (2.8× citation rate)
2. Dense factual content with specific numbers
3. FAQ schema on every landing page
4. Comparison tables (structured, extractable)
5. Explicit "what it is" paragraph in first 100 words
6. Get mentioned on Reddit and HN (Claude + Perplexity index these heavily)
7. Publish original research
8. Claim Crunchbase, LinkedIn company page, Wikidata (all feed AI training corpora)

## What NOT to Do

1. Don't pay for directory submission services ($60–$200 packages) — it's just copy-paste
2. Don't submit to spam directories (DR under 10) — dilutes backlink profile
3. Don't duplicate descriptions across directories — AI engines penalize
4. Don't submit before destination pages exist
5. Don't ask for upvotes on Product Hunt — the 2026 algorithm penalizes it
6. Don't skip G2/Capterra reviews — zero-review listings are dead
7. Don't over-index on launch-day spike — the flywheel is templates + alternatives + reviews + content
